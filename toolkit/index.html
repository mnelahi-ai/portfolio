Transformer Short Circuit Calculator

Infinite-bus and source-limited 3-phase fault at transformer secondary. Method per IEC 60909 (simplified).

Ifl = S / (√3·U). Infinite bus: Isc = Ifl × 100/Z%. With source: 1/Isc_total = 1/Isc_tx + 1/Isc_source. Motor contribution not included — add per IEC 60909 / class rules if significant.

Switchboard Rating Calculator

Busbar/incomer rating from connected load and demand factor.

I = (kW × DF) / (√3 × V × PF) × 1000. Recommended rating = next standard size ≥ I × (1+margin). Standard sizes: 630/800/1000/1250/1600/2000/2500/3200/4000/5000/6300 A (IEC 60439/61439 preferred values).

Fuel Consumption Estimation

Generator/engine fuel use from power, load factor and SFOC.

Fuel(kg) = P × LF × SFOC × h / 1000. Litres = kg / density × 1000. SFOC varies with load — use the datasheet value at the actual load point. Typical MDO density ≈ 850 kg/m³, HFO ≈ 980 kg/m³.

Motor Earth Fault Current

Earth fault current for resistance-earthed and solidly-earthed systems.

NER system: Ief ≈ (V/√3)/(Rn+Rf) — neglects cable and source impedance (conservative). Solidly earthed: Ief approaches 3-phase fault level; full calc requires zero-sequence impedances per IEC 60909-0. Protection setting typically 10–20% of NER let-through.

Transformer Full Load Current

Primary and secondary rated currents for 3-phase transformers.

I = kVA × 1000 / (√3 × V). Standard ratings per IEC 60076: 100/160/250/315/400/500/630/800/1000/1250/1600/2000/2500 kVA.

Motor Full Load Current & Trip Size

Motor FLC with suggested overload and breaker frame size.

3PH: I = P×1000/(√3×V×PF×η). 1PH: I = P×1000/(V×PF×η). Overload set ≈ 100–110% FLC; breaker = next standard rating ≥ 125% FLC (verify coordination with starter type and class rules). Always confirm against motor nameplate.

Cable Voltage Drop

3-phase / 1-phase volt drop using conductor R and X per km.

3PH: Vd = √3×I×L×(R·cosφ + X·sinφ)/1000/n. 1PH: Vd = 2×I×L×(R·cosφ + X·sinφ)/1000/n. Typical limits: 6% feeder (SOLAS/class), 3% lighting / 5% power (shore practice). Take R and X from the cable manufacturer's datasheet at conductor operating temperature (90°C for XLPE).

Switchboard / Panel Heat Dissipation

Total heat generation and ventilation airflow for electrical rooms / panels.

Q (m³/s) = P / (ρ × cp × ΔT). Component losses must come from manufacturer data (breakers, contactors, VSDs, busbars I²R). For HVAC sizing, pass total W to the mechanical discipline. Method per IEC 61439-1 thermal verification.

Protective Earth Cable Sizing (Adiabatic)

Minimum earth conductor cross-section per IEC 60364-5-54 adiabatic equation.

S = √(I²t)/k, rounded up to next standard size: 1.5/2.5/4/6/10/16/25/35/50/70/95/120/150/185/240/300 mm². k values per IEC 60364-5-54 Table 54.2 (initial 30°C). Class rules may impose minimum sizes regardless of calculation.

Power Tariff / Energy Cost

Operating energy cost from load profile and tariff rate.

Energy = kW × h × days. Cost = kWh × tariff. Enter the current tariff from your utility (e.g. SP Group for Singapore) — rates change quarterly.

Enclosure Heat Dissipation (incl. Ex e JBs / UPS enclosures)

Temperature rise of a sealed enclosure from internal heat load — natural convection/radiation.

ΔT = P/(k·A·f). Effective area A from enclosure faces (IEC 60890-style); k ≈ 5.5 W/m²K painted steel, ≈ 3.5 W/m²K GRP/polyester (verify with manufacturer). For Ex e certified enclosures the certificate's maximum dissipated power table OVERRIDES this estimate — this tool is for screening only. Terminal heat per IEC 60079-7 rated values.
