PMS Dispatch & Safety Logic

Zero-emission mode sails on BESS alone and blocks genset start until SOC reaches the 20% safety floor, then forces changeover. Hybrid mode lets the PMS peak-shave: BESS covers transients first, genset 1 starts when sustained demand exceeds BESS capability or SOC ≤ 30%, genset 2 auto-starts above 85% loading on genset 1 (N+1 headroom) and stops below 40% combined with hysteresis. Genset-only mode recharges the BESS with spare engine capacity — engines are held above 30% load to avoid wet-stacking. If demand exceeds all available sources, non-essential hotel load is shed first and propulsion is power-limited last — essential services are never shed. The insulation monitor models the unearthed IT distribution used on aluminium hulls: a first earth fault raises an alarm without tripping (continuity of service), while the crew locates the fault before a second fault can drive stray current through the hull.
