Circulating Sheath Current — Why Both-End Earthing Can Start a Fire

A single-core cable's load current induces EMF in its metallic sheath/armour. Earth the sheath at both ends and that EMF drives a circulating current around the loop — pure loss, turned into heat inside the cable. I've investigated the aftermath in the field: overheated glands, burnt terminations, fire. Choose the bonding method and watch the sheath current, losses and temperature respond.

Field Lesson

In the investigated failure, single-core armoured cables were glanded and earthed at both ends. The induced EMF drove continuous circulating current through the armour; I²R heating cooked the terminations until insulation failed and fire followed. The fix — opening one earth end (single-point bonding) — kills the loop, but the open end must be treated as live during faults: insulate the gland, fit a sheath voltage limiter (SVL), and keep the standing voltage within your safety limit (typ. ≤ 25–65 V at full load). Study before you apply.
